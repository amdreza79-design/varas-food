Kaluteh Dates

Also known as: Kali Dates · Kalooteh · Kalote

Kerman Province 14–22% Moisture Caramel-Sweet

Kaluteh dates are a highly prized variety cultivated in the Jiroft region of Kerman province. Similar to Mazafati but with a firmer structure and slightly lighter color, they ripen from vibrant red to a rich dark brown. Available both natural and lightly oiled — the oiled variety achieves a glossy premium appearance and extended shelf life. Their oval shape, meaty flesh, and distinctly caramel-like sweetness make them one of Iran's most valuable commercial export varieties. Main export markets include Tajikistan, Kazakhstan, India, Russia, the UAE and Chile. Packaged in 5 kg and 10 kg cartons to preserve quality during international transit.

Mazafati Dates

Also known as: Kimia Dates · Bam Dates

Bam, Kerman 18–35% Moisture Rich & Fudgy

Mazafati — also called Kimia or Bam dates — are Iran's crown jewel of date exports, accounting for approximately 20% of the country's total date output. Deep dark brown to near-black in color, they feature an exceptionally soft, fleshy texture with high natural moisture that delivers a uniquely fudgy, melt-in-the-mouth eating experience. Their intense sweetness carries notes of dark chocolate, caramel and molasses, making them internationally beloved for fresh consumption, gifting, and premium food applications. Available in multiple moisture grades. Bam's unique microclimate and mineral-rich soil give Mazafati an irreplaceable depth of flavor that no other region can replicate.

Zahedi Dates

Also known as: Zahidi Dates · Noble Dates

Bushehr & Khuzestan Semi-Dry Nutty-Sweet

Zahedi dates are distinguished by their beautiful golden-yellow to amber color and firm, dry texture that sets them apart from Iran's softer varieties. Their characteristic nutty sweetness with caramel undertones makes them ideal for snacking, baking, and industrial food processing. With moisture content under 14%, Zahedi dates boast a shelf life of 1–3 years — making them exceptionally well-suited for long-distance export and large-scale distribution. Rich in fiber, potassium, magnesium and B-vitamins. Grown across the warm plains of Bushehr, Khuzestan and Fars provinces. Widely exported to Europe, Southeast Asia, the Middle East and Africa.

Rare Variety

Mordasang Dates

Native to Jiroft · One of a Kind

Jiroft, Kerman Cold Nature Soft Yellow

Mordasang is one of the rarest date varieties in the world — the only date with a "cold nature" in traditional Iranian medicine. Native to the Jiroft region of Kerman province, this semi-dry variety features a soft yellowish color, flesh that separates cleanly from the pit, and a mild, delicate sweetness. Unlike many varieties, its consumption does not cause internal heat, making it prized in natural health circles. Iran produces approximately 15,000 tons annually, primarily for domestic markets. Due to its unique profile and limited availability, it is a true specialty product. Ask us about current export availability.
